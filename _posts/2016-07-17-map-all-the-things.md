There's a good reason this blog has not been updated in months. Over the past few months, three of my dreams came true: 1) I landed a JavaScript developer position at a fantastic company, 2) I was admitted to the University of Wisconsin-Madison's MS degree program in Web Map Programming (Cartography and GIS Development), and 3) I finally moved out of the suburbs and landed in the arts district in downtown Vancouver, WA, where I can walk to parks, shops, and restaurants as well as catch a convenient express bus to my wonderful new job.

There has been much rejoicing.

## New Job

A few months ago, my LinkedIn profile came to the attention of a manager looking for a new developer for his team, someone with a passion for JavaScript, mapping, and public health. Before I knew it, I was employed! The intersection of my skills and interests make this gig a perfect fit. I'm grateful every day that I get to sling code for a worthy cause with lovely, fun people who embrace learning and are as excited as I am about doing good and interesting things with data.

I'm in heaven.


## New School

As much as I am still in shock about getting such a great job, I'm in an equal amount of shock that I got into a Tier 1 university for web cartography. I plan to take one class per semester and finish three years from now. The culminating work is a capstone and practicum in lieu of a thesis, so I will graduate with a robust, advanced portfolio, one that should be informed by the work I'm doing for my company during that time. 

I get to spend my days and nights for the new few years immersed in web development, JavaScript, Python, and all things geospatial. I couldn't be more thrilled.

## New City

My husband and I moved to Washington when we fell in love with Vancouver this year. I could go on and on about how much I love my new little city. You can feel the revitalization in the air and witness it unfold before your eyes. There's so much development happening that it feels like living in a Sim city. My daily personal geography has changed in numerous ways, all for the better. I love living along the Columbia River, being two blocks from an 80,000 sq. ft. library, and exploring my new home state. 


Onward!
