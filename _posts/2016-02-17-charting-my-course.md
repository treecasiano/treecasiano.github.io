## Going Back to Graduate School
I bit the bullet and submitted an application to grad school last week. I realize now more than ever that I will only be happy if I can combine my coding skills with a profession that involves intellectual rigor, creativity, and my love of geospatial technologies. I have been slowly chipping away at an undergraduate certificate in GIS through the local community college, but it has been difficult to find the time and energy to finish it while trying to level up my web development skills. However, I am now ready to fold all my previous GIS and programming training and my lifelong love of maps into a career as a GIS developer.

I found an online accredited master's degree that focuses on cartography and web map programming. I have all the right prerequisites, and I was able to write the personal statement effortlessly - all good signs. If I am accepted, I'll share more of the details. If I don't get in, then I'll come up with an alternate plan that allows me to do more of the things I love with code. For now, I'm focused on my MEAN stack project, my work with Hack Oregon, my upcoming D3 course, Coursera's new GIS specialization, and completing Free Code Camp's tracks. This should all be good prep for grad school and/or a job, so either way, I'm on the right path.
