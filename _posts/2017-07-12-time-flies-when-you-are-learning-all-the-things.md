The last year was largely a blur between work and school. Here's why.

I completed two grad school courses: Geocomputing and Spatial Databases. The first 
was a crash course in writing scripts in a geospatial context. I dusted off my Python 
and got better acquainted with the open source GIS world. The second course introduced 
me to PostgreSQL and gave me practice designing and implementing spatial databases. I 
feel like I've only scratched the surface of all the things I need and want to know and look forward to next year's courses that all focus on cartography for the web. 

On the job I spent a great deal of time debugging and extending a complex, largely undocumented, and inadequately tested legacy Angular codebase, scrubbing the hell out of some gnarly CSS, harnessing as much code into tests as my time and understanding would allow, developing an intense appreciation for testing, honing my frustration tolerance, and writing documentation. I have whiplash from a year of nearly incessant context-switching. It has been exhausting, but I've been grateful 
for every second of it.

The next year will see me level up skills in component-based architecture as I work on 
some new Angular 1.6 projects at work. I'm hoping to spend time with React and Ruby 
on Rails, too, just to give myself a different perspective and a broader set of skills. I'd really like to restart my Make Perfect side project (a music practice app). Ha! I say this like I'll have Magical Free Time (tm). Now I know why the GitHub activity on every new 
developer drops off as soon as they take the first real dev job. Just trying to stay on
 top of the job is all-consuming. (And I'm the crazy person who has added grad school 
 to the mix.) Still, I will make 
a valiant attempt to spin up something new for my portfolio that reflects all 
the hard-won skills developed over the past year. 

Oh, and I'm moving back to Portland. There will be much rejoicing!
  
  Onward!
